Why this needs to exist

Knowledge doesn't leave the building. It just stops being findable.

Every team has lived both columns below — usually in the same week.

Without Continuum
  • Decisions live in people's heads until they leave the company
  • The same debate happens twice, six months apart
  • New hires ping five people to learn "why we did it this way"
  • Action items said out loud, never tracked anywhere
With Continuum
  • Every decision is sourced to the exact meeting and moment it was made
  • The room gets warned before it reverses itself, not after
  • "Why did we choose X" gets a sourced answer in seconds
  • Action items extracted automatically — owner and due date included
The 6 killer features

Two of these are why we win. The other four make it usable daily.

Ghost listener

Silently joins every meeting. Transcribes, extracts decisions, action items, and open questions — all in real time.

Memory weaver

Auto-links meeting output to past docs, tickets, and Slack threads. Builds a live knowledge graph per topic, project, and person.

CORE

Live context injection

During a meeting, Continuum whispers relevant context into the sidebar — "This contradicts the Q2 decision. See: [link]."

Org memory search

"Why did we drop Feature X?" — ask a question in plain English, get the exact meeting, who said what, and what was decided.

Meeting ghost mode

Ask it clarifying questions yourself from the sidebar when something's ambiguous or contradictory. It answers in the panel — never out loud, never interrupts.

CORE

Decision drift tracker

Detects when the team reverses a past decision without acknowledging it. Flags it silently and links straight back to the original context.

The X-factor

It's not a summary. It's a diff.

Continuum doesn't end a meeting with "here's what was said." It ends with what changed in the org's memory — what was added, what was reinforced, and what now contradicts something already on record.

That's the reframe: this isn't a notetaker with a search bar bolted on. It's the system of record for how your company's mind changes, meeting over meeting.

⚠Payments vendor — new statement contradicts active decision

Data model at a glance

What the graph is actually made of.

Organization

Top-level tenant. Owns teams, retention policy, plan tier.

Team

Scopes access. Every meeting and node belongs to one.

Meeting

A session: participants, platform, start/end, status.

Decision

Claim, owner, status — active, superseded, or reversed.

KnowledgeNode

Person, project, customer, or tool — anything mentioned.

ActionItem

Owner, due date, status — extracted, not typed.

ContradictionFlag

Links two conflicting decisions plus a resolution state.

Document

Uploaded docs — chunked and embedded like a transcript.

Roles & access

Memory follows org structure, not convenience.

org_admin
Priya

Provisions teams, sets retention policy, sees the audit log. Owns governance, not just usage.

member
Devon

Lives in meetings. Gets the sidebar in calls, full search and graph access scoped to their teams.

Only two roles exist. Anyone on a call without an account — a candidate, a vendor, a client — is never issued a login or token. They're tagged is_external at the transcript level, not granted a role.
MVP scope

What's real for the demo vs. what's roadmap.

Building for the demo
  • Live capture → embed → graph pipeline, end to end
  • Seeded decision history to contradict against
  • One rehearsed, reliable live contradiction catch
  • Search + graph view, enough to verify sources live
  • Team-scoped auth across admin / member, plus external-speaker tagging
Building after
  • Platform-certified Zoom / Teams / Meet apps
  • Multi-language transcription
  • Per-node ACLs beyond team-level scoping
  • Slack / email digests, self-tuning drift thresholds
